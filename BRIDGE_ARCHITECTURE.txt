║                   LANGUAGE & TONE — GUIDING PRINCIPLE                       ║
╚═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

Every piece of text follows the "Warmth Standard":

   Read aloud. If it sounds like documentation,
   a config file, or an academic paper → REWRITE

✅ WARM (sounds like a calm colleague)
   • "Ready to connect?"
   • "Your board is safe"
   • "See your board layout live"
   • "Changes only when you ask"

❌ COLD (sounds like a form or manual)
   • "Instantiate WebSocket bridge"
   • "WARNING: Data loss potential"
   • "Enable bidirectional sync"
   • "Confirm operation execution"

╔═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║                          KEY INSIGHT                                         ║
╚═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

                    The system decides.
                    The user overrides.

Bridge is smart enough to NOT force connection on users.
But when user CHOOSES to connect, we make it clear what's happening.
Trust comes from transparency, not from hiding complexity.

User sees:
  • What will happen ("See board layout live")
  • Why it's safe ("Your board is safe")
  • What they control ("Changes only when you ask")
  • How to undo ("You can undo them in KiCad")

Result: Confident user who trusts the tool.
